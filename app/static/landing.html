What the agent actually does

Four stages, in order — each one closes a gap that makes teams nervous about putting AI in front of customers.

  1. 1.0 — ANSWER

    It answers from your docs, not its imagination.

    Questions are answered with retrieval-augmented generation over your own help content, so replies cite a real source instead of hallucinating. If the docs don't cover it, the agent says so and escalates.

  2. 2.0 — ACT

    It does the work, not just the talking.

    The agent calls real tools on your systems — scoped to one customer's data at a time.

    • lookupLive order & shipment status
    • ticketFiles a support ticket
    • emailSends a customer email
    • escalateHands off to a human

  3. 3.0 — GATE

    It can't move money on its own.

    Refunds are never issued by the model. They're held for one-click human approval, capped at the order total, and processed exactly once — so the AI can't refund incorrectly, and a retry can't double-refund.

  4. 4.0 — ACCOUNT

    Every call's cost in plain sight.

    Token usage, latency, and dollar cost are recorded for every AI call and rolled up per model and per day. Spend is a number you can read, not a surprise on the invoice.
